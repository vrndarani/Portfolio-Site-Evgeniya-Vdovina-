Here is our design process from scratch to a finished (although nothing is ever fully finished in design world) prototype.

My Design Process



Research

We wanted to solve the problem of people having immediate access to verified experts in various fields by building an app where users can quickly find, call and text experts in the required field. After realizing the scope of the app, we moved on to competitive analysis that highlighted some market opportunities for us. As the users of expert apps tend to be younger ones, they can be reached by larger efforts in marketing, especially SMM on Instagram and TikTok. Another thing we discovered is there is no app that provides both top verified experts and immediate access to them. With that in mind, we deciced to introduce online/offline presence and rating system that also includes experts’ availability. We conducted potential user interviews and collected their needs and goals that proved our problem statement to be correct.





Ideation

We created a user persona to give a face to our users’ needs and goals, analyses their task flow and designed their user flow. This helped us see what must be included in the app and eventually build a sitemap with the right hierarchy.





Wireframe

These low-fidelity wireframes are meant to organize the information on each screen of the app for further design process: mid- and high- levels of fidelity and prototyping.





Design

With several rounds of iterations based on user testing and peers’ feedback, we created this high-fidelity wireframes that are to be used in prototyping. In the process we focused on providing all the necessary information and avoiding clutter. We added online status to experts, changed buttons locations, using the feedback from user testing and our observations.





Prototyping

After 3 rounds of user testing, 2 rounds of A/B testing, we made iterations and finalized the prototype (although no project can be final and there is room for improvement here as well). We worked on final frictions and tried to improve accessibility with color choices and grouping.
The prototype we created can be found here



Conclusion

The fascinating discoveries we made at research stage - of how people act and what they need when looking for help - allowed us to create an app that smoothly and easily brings the user to their goal: to quickly find the right expert, connect with them right away - through calls or messages.
